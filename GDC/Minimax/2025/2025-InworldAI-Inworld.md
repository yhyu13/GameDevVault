# Inworld AI — Inworld for UGC and Production

## TL;DR

Inworld AI provides **LLM-driven NPC and character-generation tools** for game studios. Their GDC 2025 talk split into two tracks: (1) "AI characters" as in-game NPCs, (2) "AI characters" as UGC tools that let players author characters.

## What the talk covered

- The Inworld product:
  - Configurable LLM-backed character "brains" with personality, memory, and goals.
  - Voice synthesis with character-consistent timbre.
  - Multi-language support.
- **Production use cases** (creator-focused):
  - Indie studios drop Inworld into their game for low-cost NPC dialogue.
  - Studios use Inworld's **character-creation UGC tools** so players can author and share characters.
- **UGC platform partnerships**: Inworld is integrated with several UGC platforms where players can spawn AI characters into their experiences.
- The **safety** discussion: Inworld characters run on policy-filtered LLMs. Players can't easily prompt the character into harmful content.

## Why it matters for game creation

- Inworld represents the **"AI as content, not as system"** view. The AI character is an asset the player can author, the same as a 3D model.
- For studios that want LLM-driven characters without building the infrastructure, Inworld is the off-the-shelf option.
- The UGC-platform integrations are the more interesting story: player-authored AI characters in a game create an entirely new content category.

## Why it's borderline

- Strictly, "AI NPC" is in-game AI, not a creation tool.
- Included because the **player-UGC story** is on-topic: AI characters as authored content is a creation-tool story.
