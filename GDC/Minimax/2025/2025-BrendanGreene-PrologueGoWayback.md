# Brendan Greene — Prologue: Go Wayback! (AI Generated Maps)

## TL;DR

Brendan Greene (the creator of PUBG) revealed his new project: **Prologue: Go Wayback!**, a single-player survival game where the world is **generated in real time by an AI model**. The first time a high-profile game director has shipped a project where the entire playable environment is model-generated.

## What the talk covered

- The project: a meditative single-player survival game. The world is procedurally generated by a machine-learning model, terrain + atmosphere + weather + biomes.
- The generation pipeline:
  - ML model trained on a large corpus of real-world terrain + satellite + elevation data.
  - At runtime, the model generates a new world per playthrough.
  - The player walks through and survives; the world has no pre-authored locations.
- The design philosophy: a world the player gets lost in, with no two runs the same.
- The honest caveats: technical limits on detail; the model hallucinates weird terrain features; QA is hard when the world is different every time.

## Why it matters for game creation

- Brendan Greene is one of the most influential game designers alive. **He is publicly betting his post-PUBG career on AI-generated worlds**. This is a signal of where the industry thinks the next 5 years go.
- For game designers, "design for a world the player gets lost in" is a different discipline from "design for a world the player maps." The role of the designer shifts from author to curator.
- For technical teams, the QA problem (every run is different) is unsolved. The talk acknowledged this is an open research problem.

## Limitations

- The ML model is terrain + atmosphere, not gameplay logic. AI doesn't generate quests, NPCs, or story.
- The generated worlds are visually compelling but gameplay-shallow. The "what do you do" is still survival loop.
- The hallucination problem is real — weird terrain features that don't make gameplay sense.
