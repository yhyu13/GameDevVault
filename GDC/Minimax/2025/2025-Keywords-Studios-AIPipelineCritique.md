# Keywords Studios — AI in the Production Pipeline

## TL;DR

The most-quoted "skeptic" talk of GDC 2025. Keywords Studios — one of the largest game-services companies (localisation, QA, art, engineering) — pushed back on the AI hype with a simple message: **"too many people thinking about better AI, too few thinking about better products."**

## What the talk covered

- The thesis: AI demos are everywhere. **AI in a shipping production pipeline** is rare. The gap is operational, not technical.
- The talk catalogued the failure modes they've seen across 100+ projects:
  - **Quality variance**: AI output quality swings wildly. The team spends more time reviewing AI output than they saved generating it.
  - **Tool sprawl**: every team tries a different tool; no shared standards.
  - **Legal ambiguity**: who's liable for AI-generated content in a contract dispute?
  - **Artist rejection**: artists refuse to use AI tools that produce low-quality output, even if the tools "save time."
- The recommendations:
  - **Pick one or two AI tools, master them, then add a third.**
  - **Measure the total cost, not just the tool cost.** Review, fixing, and integration time often exceeds the generation savings.
  - **Define your AI policy before you start using AI tools.** A clear, written policy prevents legal and HR surprises.
  - **Treat AI as a junior team member**: it's fast but needs supervision.
- The closing line: "AI is a tool, not a strategy. The strategy is the game."

## Why it matters for game creation

- This is the **most-cited industry-voice talk** warning against AI hype. For every "AI will replace X" headline, this talk is the counterweight.
- For studios that are early in their AI adoption, the practical advice (one tool, measure total cost, write a policy) is more valuable than any demo.
- The "artist rejection" point is the under-reported one. Studios that mandate AI tools often see artist morale drop and quality drop with it.

## What it didn't cover

- Specific tool recommendations. The talk was about process, not products.
